# Moscow Real Estate Market Analysis (2020–2026)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes Moscow’s real estate market from 2020 to 2026 using interactive Power BI dashboards. The analysis focuses on new-build and rental properties, metro accessibility, housing segments, developer activity, and pricing trends to generate business insights and support data-driven decision-making.

---

# Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze Moscow real estate market trends using property sales, rental prices, metro accessibility, mortgage rates, housing categories, and district-level data to identify key market patterns and generate meaningful business insights.

---

# Dashboard Pages

## 1. New Build Market Analysis

This dashboard focuses on Moscow’s new-build residential market and includes:

* Property price trends
* Metro accessibility impact
* Premium vs economy housing comparison
* Top expensive districts
* Developer market share analysis
* Mortgage rate impact analysis

## 2. Rental Market Analysis

This dashboard focuses on Moscow’s rental housing market and includes:

* Rental price trends
* Rental price comparison by metro accessibility

# Key Insights

* Properties located near metro stations recorded higher property and rental prices compared to properties farther from metro accessibility zones.

* Premium districts such as Arbat, Tverskoy, Yakimanka, and Presnensky consistently showed the highest property and rental prices.

* Premium and business-class housing segments recorded significantly higher average prices compared to economy-class housing.

* A small number of developers dominated the Moscow new-build market, contributing to a large share of total property listings.

* Building types such as Stalin and Monolith apartments showed higher rental prices compared to panel and Khrushchev-style buildings.

* Moscow’s real estate market showed an overall upward trend between 2020 and 2026, indicating continuous market growth over time.
